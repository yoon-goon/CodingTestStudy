The Adventures of Tom Sawyer 
Mark Twain

Chapter 1 
The old lady pulled her spectacles down and looked over them about the room;
then she put them up and looked out under them. She seldom
or never looked through them for so small a thing as a boy; 
they were her state pair, the pride of her heart, and were built 
for "style," not service-she could have seen through a pair of stove lids 
just as well. She looked perplexed for a
moment, and then said, not fiercely, but still loud enough for
the furniture to hear:
